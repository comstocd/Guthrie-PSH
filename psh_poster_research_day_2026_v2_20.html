Background & Problem Statement

Guthrie Lourdes Hospital's new Ambulatory Surgery Center (ASC) joins an established perioperative infrastructure that includes the Joint Academy — a system-wide structured joint replacement education program with procedure-specific patient workbooks. This is a strong institutional foundation with significant opportunity for enhancement.

The current pathway, however, operates through fragmented entry points: Joint Education classes are initiated by Central Scheduling 3–6 weeks pre-operatively; pre-admission testing and medical clearance are coordinated through individual orthopedic offices 2–3 weeks before surgery — leaving insufficient time for meaningful optimization. Medication protocols vary by surgeon. PT/OT arrangements are deferred to the day of discharge. Patient-reported gaps include erroneous medication hold instructions and incorrect arrival times, reflecting a system without a unifying coordinating throughline.

Opportunity A structured Perioperative Surgical Home (PSH) — an evidence-based, physician-led model endorsed by the American Society of Anesthesiologists (ASA) and aligned with American College of Surgeons perioperative quality initiatives — addresses these gaps by extending and systematizing the existing Joint Academy infrastructure: adding a unified referral entry point, a 6–8 week optimization window, navigator-led continuity, and standardized patient communication from surgical decision through PCP handoff.

Target Population Orthopedic patients age ≥55 undergoing elective THA, TKA, or related procedures at the Guthrie Lourdes ASC. This cohort carries the highest comorbidity burden and greatest potential for structured optimization.

The PSH Model: Three-Phase Framework
1
Preoperative Optimization Clinic (6–8 wks pre-op)
Structured intake using validated tools: STOP-BANG ≥3, RCRI, Clinical Frailty Scale. Targets glycemic control (HbA1c <8.5%11,13), anemia correction, OSA management, polypharmacy review, nutritional screening (MNA-SF), PT/OT functional baseline evaluation (mobility, strength, and ADL assessment to anchor discharge planning and outcome tracking), and cardiac risk stratification per 2024 ACC/AHA stepwise algorithm (DASI → NT-proBNP → judicious stress testing). Anticoagulant/antiplatelet hold protocols and TXA eligibility screening initiated. The 6–8 week window enables optimization not achievable within the current 2–3 week pre-admission testing timeline.
2
Intraoperative Coordination
Anesthesia team pre-briefed via the structured preoperative summary note on OSA status, airway flags, anticoagulation plan, and ERAS pathway. Surgical team and PACU/postoperative nursing receive discipline-specific sections of the same note. Surgical-specific checklists embedded in Epic. TXA administered per AAHKS/AAOS consensus protocol10 where not contraindicated.
3
Postoperative Recovery & Reintegration (4–6 wks)
Multimodal, opioid-sparing pain management (>50% opioid reduction vs. conventional care17). Early mobilization and PT/OT protocols. Risk-stratified VTE prophylaxis per 2022 ICM-VTE and AAOS guidelines9. CAM delirium screening with HELP protocol components for high-risk patients. Beers Criteria medication review. Coordinated PCP handoff with comprehensive discharge documentation.

Evidence Base Systematic reviews encompassing >216,000 TJA patients confirm ERAS reduces LOS in 95.8% of studies with cost savings demonstrated in 85.7%; meta-analysis confirms LOS reductions of 1–3 days with significantly fewer complications versus conventional care.14,15 Benefits are sustained in the older patient cohort that constitutes the PSH target population.16 At $19,600 per averted complication,1 the financial case for structured preoperative optimization is well-established.

Implementation Architecture
Referral & Enrollment Pathway
Initiated at surgical scheduling via Epic order set. Minimum 6–8 week lead time. Eligible: all elective THA/TKA patients age ≥55 at the Guthrie Lourdes ASC.
Patient Navigator Program
Assigned at referral; single point of contact from preoperative evaluation through PCP handoff. Coordinates appointments, tracks optimization milestones, flags gaps to the perioperative internist, and proactively notifies all involved departments of clinical schedule changes. Transmits a structured discharge summary to the PCP.
Standardized SmartForm Intake (Epic)
Risk scores, labs, optimization flags, and anesthesia briefing fields embedded in Epic workflow; Care Everywhere for external record retrieval
Comprehensive Preoperative Lab Panel
CBC, CMP with eGFR (CKD staging — Stage ≥3 triggers nephrology co-management consideration5), HbA1c, fructosamine (≤293 µmol/L threshold), coagulation profile, serum albumin, TLC, transferrin; ECG and chest X-ray as indicated
Medication Optimization Protocol
Anticoagulant bridging per bleeding risk; CKD dose adjustment (apixaban preferred over LMWH in Stage III (rivaroxaban: avoid CrCl <30); Stage IV–V: LMWH/UFH per nephrology). NSAIDs contraindicated in CKD ≥3. Beers Criteria review; insulin management; polypharmacy triage.
Patient Blood Management (PBM)
Preop anemia optimization (iron, EPO where indicated) + TXA screening per AAHKS/AAOS/Hip & Knee Societies consensus guideline
Nutritional & Frailty Assessment
MNA-SF primary screen; biochemical anchors: albumin, TLC, transferrin; CFS ≥58 triggers prehabilitation referral; delirium–malnutrition link explicitly flagged
PT/OT Functional Baseline Evaluation
Documents mobility, strength, and ADL function at initial preop visit. Establishes objective discharge criteria (home vs. SNF) and enables pre-to-post functional comparison at 6 and 12 months. Post-acute PT/OT arrangements confirmed prior to surgery.
Structured Preoperative Summary Note
Synthesizes all preoperative data from Epic EMR and CareEverywhere at optimization completion. AI-assisted summarization supplements clinician review to improve comprehensiveness and flag potential perioperative complications. Structured with discipline-specific sections for surgeons, anesthesiologists, PACU nursing, and hospitalists.
Patient Education & Prehabilitation Counseling
Builds on Joint Academy workbooks: prehabilitation protocol, VTE prevention, expected recovery trajectory, and opioid stewardship. SDOH screening for social barriers. Discharge criteria reviewed preoperatively.
Governance & Quality Oversight
Steering committee co-chaired by perioperative medicine and orthopedic surgery; includes anesthesiology, pharmacy, nursing, and PT/OT. Quarterly PDSA cycles; protocol revision at ≥10% benchmark deviation. Annual CMO report.

Key Risk Stratification Tools
STOP-BANG (OSA Screening)
Score ≥3:3 High risk for moderate-severe OSA → formal evaluation; CPAP optimization pre-op if OSA confirmed or clinically indicated  |  Score ≥5: High probability severe OSA → urgent CPAP titration + anesthesia alert. (Chung F et al., Anesthesiology 2008)
RCRI / 2024 ACC/AHA Stepwise Algorithm
RCRI ≥2 or MACE risk ≥1%2 triggers: DASI functional assessment → NT-proBNP → judicious stress testing as indicated. (Thompson et al., Circulation 2024)
Clinical Frailty Scale (CFS)
CFS ≥58 (moderate-to-severe frailty): multidisciplinary prehabilitation, nutritional support, social work engagement, enhanced postoperative monitoring and delirium prevention protocols
Glycemic Threshold: HbA1c <8.5%
Per UK JBDS perioperative guidelines; reflects ~90-day mean blood glucose based on RBC lifespan. Fructosamine ≤293 µmol/L used when HbA1c unreliable (hemoglobinopathy, hemolysis, recent transfusion)4. (Shohat et al., Bone Joint J 2019)

Conclusions & Next Steps

With the Guthrie Lourdes ASC now operational, PSH implementation is intentionally staged to maximize scientific rigor: the ASC's inaugural year serves as a prospective baseline, enabling robust pre/post comparison against both pre-ASC surgical volumes and the unoptimized ASC cohort. The PSH extends and systematizes the existing Joint Academy infrastructure — adding navigator-led continuity, a standardized 6–8 week optimization window, and unified patient communication where fragmentation currently exists.

  • Evidence-aligned: Structured on ERAS principles, ACC/AHA perioperative guidelines, and joint society VTE/TXA consensus guidance
  • Methodologically rigorous: Prospective baseline year (2026) enables pre/post and ASC vs. pre-ASC comparisons — strengthening QI reporting and future publication
  • Scalable: Orthopedic pilot with clear expansion pathway to general surgery, spine, and other high-volume elective service lines
  • Academically enriching: Baseline data collection, metric design, and Epic infrastructure built during observation year
