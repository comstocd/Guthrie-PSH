Background & Problem Statement

Guthrie Lourdes Hospital's new Ambulatory Surgery Center (ASC) joins an established perioperative infrastructure that includes the Joint Academy — a system-wide structured joint replacement education program with procedure-specific patient workbooks. This is a strong institutional foundation with significant opportunity for enhancement.

The current pathway, however, operates through fragmented entry points: Joint Education classes are initiated by Central Scheduling 3–6 weeks pre-operatively; pre-admission testing and medical clearance are coordinated through individual orthopedic offices 2–3 weeks before surgery — leaving insufficient time for meaningful optimization. Medication protocols vary by surgeon. PT/OT arrangements are deferred to the day of discharge. Patient-reported gaps include erroneous medication hold instructions and incorrect arrival times, reflecting a system without a unifying coordinating throughline.

Opportunity A structured Perioperative Surgical Home (PSH) — an evidence-based, physician-led model endorsed by the American Society of Anesthesiologists (ASA) and aligned with American College of Surgeons perioperative quality initiatives — addresses these gaps by extending and systematizing the existing Joint Academy infrastructure: adding a unified referral entry point, a 6–8 week optimization window, navigator-led continuity, and standardized patient communication from surgical decision through PCP handoff.

Target Population Orthopedic patients age ≥55 undergoing elective THA, TKA, or related procedures at the Guthrie Lourdes ASC. This cohort carries the highest comorbidity burden and greatest potential for structured optimization.

The PSH Model: Three-Phase Framework
1
Preoperative Optimization Clinic (6–8 wks pre-op)
Structured intake using validated tools: STOP-BANG ≥3, RCRI, Clinical Frailty Scale. Targets glycemic control (HbA1c <8.5%), anemia correction, OSA management, polypharmacy review, nutritional screening (MNA-SF), PT/OT functional baseline evaluation (mobility, strength, and ADL assessment to anchor discharge planning and outcome tracking), and cardiac risk stratification per 2024 ACC/AHA stepwise algorithm (DASI → NT-proBNP → judicious stress testing). Anticoagulant/antiplatelet hold protocols and TXA eligibility screening initiated. The 6–8 week window enables optimization not achievable within the current 2–3 week pre-admission testing timeline.
2
Intraoperative Coordination
Anesthesia team pre-briefed via the structured preoperative summary note on OSA status, airway flags, anticoagulation plan, and ERAS pathway. Surgical team and PACU/postoperative nursing receive discipline-specific sections of the same note. Surgical-specific checklists embedded in Epic. TXA administered per AAHKS/AAOS/ASRA consensus protocol where not contraindicated.
3
Postoperative Recovery & Reintegration (4–6 wks)
Multimodal, opioid-sparing pain management. Early mobilization and PT/OT protocols. Risk-stratified VTE prophylaxis per 2022 ICM-VTE and AAOS guidelines. CAM delirium screening with HELP protocol components for high-risk patients. Beers Criteria medication review. Coordinated PCP handoff with comprehensive discharge documentation.

Evidence Base PSH and ERAS (Enhanced Recovery After Surgery) models in comparable orthopedic programs have demonstrated LOS reductions of 1–3 days (inpatient orthopedic programs) and up to 48% reduction in postoperative complication rates versus conventional care (Healy MA et al., JAMA Surgery 2016).

Implementation Architecture
Referral & Enrollment Pathway
PSH referral initiated by the surgeon's office at time of surgical scheduling; target pathway automates enrollment via Epic order set triggered at booking. Minimum lead time 6–8 weeks pre-procedure. Eligible population: all elective THA/TKA/related orthopedic patients age ≥55 at the Guthrie Lourdes ASC.
Patient Navigator Program
A dedicated navigator is assigned at referral and serves as the patient's single point of contact from preoperative evaluation through PCP handoff. Coordinates appointments, tracks optimization milestones, and flags incomplete items to the perioperative internist. Proactively notifies all involved departments of any schedule changes arising from clinical considerations. Transmits a structured summary to the PCP at discharge — replacing the erroneous medication instructions and arrival communications that patients have reported when navigating the current multi-office model.
Standardized SmartForm Intake (Epic)
Risk scores, labs, optimization flags, and anesthesia briefing fields embedded in Epic workflow; Care Everywhere for external record retrieval
Comprehensive Preoperative Lab Panel
CBC, CMP with eGFR (CKD staging — Stage ≥3 triggers nephrology co-management consideration), HbA1c, fructosamine (≤293 µmol/L threshold), coagulation profile, serum albumin, TLC, transferrin; ECG and chest X-ray as indicated
Medication Optimization Protocol
Anticoagulant/antiplatelet bridging per procedure-specific bleeding risk, with renal dose adjustment for CKD (rivaroxaban/apixaban preferred over LMWH in Stage III–IV; UFH in Stage V); NSAIDs contraindicated in CKD ≥3. Beers Criteria review; antibiotic prophylaxis renally adjusted; insulin management; polypharmacy triage
Patient Blood Management (PBM)
Preop anemia optimization (iron, EPO where indicated) + TXA screening per AAHKS/AAOS/ASRA/Hip & Knee Societies consensus guideline
Nutritional & Frailty Assessment
MNA-SF primary screen; biochemical anchors: albumin, TLC, transferrin; CFS ≥5 triggers prehabilitation referral; delirium–malnutrition link explicitly flagged
PT/OT Functional Baseline Evaluation
PT/OT assessment at initial preop visit documents mobility, strength, and ADL function. Establishes objective discharge planning criteria (home vs. SNF) and enables pre-to-post functional outcome comparison at 6 and 12 months. All post-acute PT/OT arrangements confirmed prior to surgery — in contrast to the current standard of deferring these arrangements to the day of discharge.
Structured Preoperative Summary Note
At completion of the optimization period, a comprehensive medical note is generated synthesizing all preoperative data — drawing from the Guthrie Epic EMR and CareEverywhere records from outside institutions. AI-assisted history generation tools — including Epic's integrated AI summarization capabilities and available third-party options — supplement clinician synthesis to improve comprehensiveness and support identification of potential perioperative complications that might otherwise be missed in manual record review. The note is structured with discipline-specific sections tailored to the surgeons, anesthesiologists, PACU nursing staff, postoperative nurses, and hospitalists — each receiving the clinical information most relevant to their role in the patient's perioperative care.
Patient Education & Prehabilitation Counseling
Builds on the existing Joint Academy workbooks with deeper engagement: prehabilitation exercise protocol, VTE prevention, expected recovery trajectory, and opioid stewardship expectations. SDOH screening integrated at the initial visit to identify and address social barriers to optimization and recovery. Long-term goal: instilling durable healthy lifestyle practices that the primary care provider can continue building upon well beyond recovery. Written discharge criteria provided and reviewed preoperatively.
Governance & Quality Oversight
Steering committee co-chaired by perioperative medicine and orthopedic surgery; members include anesthesiology, pharmacy, nursing, PT/OT, and social work. Quarterly PDSA review cycles assess outcomes against baseline metrics; protocol revision triggered by ≥10% deviation from established outcome benchmarks. Annual report to Surgery and Medicine department leadership and CMO.

Key Risk Stratification Tools
STOP-BANG (OSA Screening)
Score ≥3: High risk for moderate-severe OSA → formal evaluation; CPAP optimization pre-op if OSA confirmed or clinically indicated  |  Score ≥5: High probability severe OSA → urgent CPAP titration + anesthesia alert. (Chung F et al., Anesthesiology 2008)
RCRI / 2024 ACC/AHA Stepwise Algorithm
RCRI ≥2 or MACE risk ≥1% triggers: DASI functional assessment → NT-proBNP → judicious stress testing as indicated. (Thompson et al., Circulation 2024)
Clinical Frailty Scale (CFS)
CFS ≥5 (moderate-to-severe frailty): multidisciplinary prehabilitation, nutritional support, social work engagement, enhanced postoperative monitoring and delirium prevention protocols
Glycemic Threshold: HbA1c <8.5%
Per UK JBDS perioperative guidelines; correlates with 90-day mean RBC lifespan. Fructosamine ≤293 µmol/L used when HbA1c unreliable (hemoglobinopathy, hemolysis, recent transfusion). (Shohat et al., Bone Joint J 2019)

Conclusions & Next Steps

With the Guthrie Lourdes ASC now operational, PSH implementation is intentionally staged to maximize scientific rigor: the ASC's inaugural year serves as a prospective baseline, enabling robust pre/post comparison against both pre-ASC surgical volumes and the unoptimized ASC cohort. The PSH extends and systematizes the existing Joint Academy infrastructure — adding navigator-led continuity, a standardized 6–8 week optimization window, and unified patient communication where fragmentation currently exists.

  • Evidence-aligned: Structured on ERAS principles, ACC/AHA perioperative guidelines, and joint society VTE/TXA consensus guidance
  • Methodologically rigorous: Prospective baseline year (2026) enables pre/post and ASC vs. pre-ASC comparisons — strengthening QI reporting and future publication
  • Scalable: Orthopedic pilot with clear expansion pathway to general surgery, spine, and other high-volume elective service lines
  • Academically enriching: Baseline data collection, metric design, and Epic infrastructure built during observation year
